Understanding Parkinson's Disease

Parkinson's Disease is a progressive neurological disorder that affects movement. It develops gradually, sometimes starting with a barely noticeable tremor in just one hand. While tremors are common, the disorder also commonly causes stiffness or slowing of movement.

Common Symptoms

Tremors

Shaking that begins in a limb, often your hand or fingers. May occur when your muscles are at rest.

Slowed Movement

Over time, Parkinson's disease may slow your movement, making simple tasks difficult and time-consuming.

Rigid Muscles

Muscle stiffness may occur in any part of your body, limiting your range of motion and causing pain.

Impaired Balance

Your posture may become stooped, or you may have balance problems as a result of Parkinson's disease.

Voice Changes in Parkinson's

Voice changes are common in Parkinson's Disease and can include:

  • Softer speech (hypophonia)
  • Monotone voice
  • Hoarse or breathy voice quality
  • Reduced pitch range
  • Imprecise articulation

These voice changes can be early indicators of the disease and are often used in diagnosis.

Treatment Options

While there is no cure for Parkinson's Disease, various treatments can help manage symptoms:

  • Medications to increase dopamine levels
  • Physical therapy to improve balance and flexibility
  • Speech therapy to address voice changes
  • Deep brain stimulation in advanced cases
  • Lifestyle modifications and exercise
